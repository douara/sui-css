<Docs.Section>
	<p>
		You can modify your layout spacing in two ways: sides and top. The sides
		spacing modifier will add some padding to the left and right of the main
		container; On the other hand, the top spacing will add some padding to
		the top of the main container, making sure it doesn't look glued to the
		WordPress top bar.
	</p>
	<p>
		The spacing added through the modifiers relies on the three main
		breakpoints from which we build our design system: default, medium, and
		large.
	</p>

		applies to every screen size; Once the screen reaches a minimum width of
		1024px, the spacing overwrites to 24px; Finally, for screens larger than
		1200px, the spacing would be 32px.
	</p>
	<Docs.Table2 head={['', 'Min container width', 'Spacing']}>
		<tr>

			<td>16px</td>
		</tr>
		<tr>
			<th scope="row">Medium</th>

			<td>24px</td>
		</tr>
		<tr>
			<th scope="row">Large</th>
			<td>≥ 1200px</td>
			<td>32px</td>
		</tr>
	</Docs.Table2>
	<p>
		All these sizes respect the 8px base gutter, following our design
		guidelines.
	</p>
</Docs.Section>
